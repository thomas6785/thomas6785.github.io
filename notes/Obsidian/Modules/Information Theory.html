Introduction

Claude Shannon developed the theory of information during WWII (wasn't published till after the war)

Seminal paper: A mathematical theory of communication

According to Shannon

Information

Information is the ability to distinguish reliably among possible alternative messages

i.e. one of many possibilities is being encoded

There is a greater than 95% chance of at least 1 bit error occurring in 4 GB of memory in a 72h period at sea level - error detection and correction is important!

Most basic error correction scheme:

  • Send every symbol three times and take the majority
  • This is reasonably effective but not at all foolproof, and extremely inefficient

Every noisy channel has a 'capacity' (C) which defines the limit on the 'rate' of code (i.e. the ratio of original message to encoded message)
(C is dimensionless, not bits/second, confusing name though right?)
