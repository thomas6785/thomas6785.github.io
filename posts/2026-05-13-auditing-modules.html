Did you know you can sit in on classes in your university for free?
Most universities allow students to 'audit' classes, allowing you to attend lectures and access course materials, but not sit the assessments, and not affect your GPA. This is an excellent way of learning something new in a low-pressure environment.
(The term 'audit' sometimes confuses people, this is not a way of evaluating the module to provide feedback)

I recently remembered that when it's not causing me endless stress and sleepless nights, I actually quite enjoy learning new things! For my final year of college I've been auditing two classes: Information Security (in Autumn) and Advanced Information Security (in Spring). These excellent classes were delivered by Prof. Felix Balado. They follow from a previous class I took in information theory, delving into cryptography from a distinctly mathematical angle.

I've been interested in cryptography since I read the Wikipedia article for AES when I was 13 (and understood next to none of it), but I've never had an opportunity to study it at a college level. I thoroughly enjoyed auditing these courses. I had been concerned that I would be too busy to keep up with the extra courseload, but when you have no assessments to complete, it's really only a couple hours a week of lecture time, which anybody can spare. Plus, at least in my university, it appears on your transcript, albeit as non-credit-bearing.
