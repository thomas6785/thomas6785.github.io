On Thursday I participated in my first Hackathon and had a great time. It was hosted by UCD's Innovation Academy and only four hours long. I was placed in a team with five strangers and given a prompt to examine how we can facilitate *future-proof learning* at our university.

I really enjoyed suggesting an idea I'd been thinking about for a while, which we continued to develop as a team.

It was amazing to work with five fellow nerds: Aditya Bhargav Akella, Blessed Rita Fonkoh, Bhuvann V, Krishna Teja, Oliver FitzPatrick.


### Sabbko
Our product was an educational platform for licensing to universities. It would include a library of learning materials grouped into modules. 
A consortium of universities and colleges around the world would produce this content for Sabbko to be licensed out to other, smaller universities.
Basically, imagine Coursera, but with three **important** differences:

#### Adaptation to Learning Style
Each 'topic' will not be taught by a single video lecture - instead, we'll commission ten or more versions of each topic in different formats (video, podcast, text, interaction experience, anything).

Each student's unique learning style will be ascertained - initially by a questionnaire, then further understood by feedback they provide while using the platform.

After a year or two of a student's studies, Sabbko will have a strong idea of their exact learning style, and tailor the content it provides to suit this.

#### Backed by a real university
Unlike Coursera, Sabbko courses would be licensed by real, in-person, prestigious universities, and given out to their students. They are intended as a supplement to the college experience, not a replacement. You will still have professors to consult, the social experience of a university, and the prestige of a real, accredited degree. Exams and assessment will be handled by your university, not be Sabbko.

#### Simply Better Videos
The likes of Coursera and Khan Academy provide reasonably good voiceover videos - significantly better than anything ever provided by my lecturers. However, it doesn't even come close to the engaging, easily-understood and exciting content you get from educational YouTube channels like Veritasium, Kurzgesagt, or Numberphile.

Sabbko content would be scripted by subject-matter experts working alongside education specialists, then animated, produced, and edited by professionals to create engaging and understandable content.

---

If lecturers were willing to rely on the Sabbko curriculum, they could drastically reduce their own teaching hours (possibly from 3-5 hours/class/week to a 1 hour/class/week check-in), allowing the university to save money (or increase class size). We believe Sabbko is potentially extremely valuable to a university and they should be willing to pay a substantial license fee. Costs include:
- Commissioning/creating new lecture content (CapEx)
- Building and maintaining the user platform and recommender algorithms
- Building, maintaining, and reviewing the curricula regularly
- Cloud space to host the lecture content
- Misc. business services

We had only a few hours to develop this idea and build a demo and presentation. I was lucky enough to be asked by my team to lead the presentation, and hope I did a good idea of pitching it to the other participants.
